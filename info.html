The cypress tree

Characteristics: Death, accidents, and sorrow
Greek Myth Interpretation: Cyparissus accidentally kills stag
Book Connection: Nanny accidentally kills Janie's naive dreams of a pear tree by enforcing that she marry Logan. She dies in peace knowing (incorrectly) that Janie will lead a stable life with Logan

The pine tree

Characteristics: longevity and peace
Greek Myth interpretation: Attis was turned into an immortal pine after being taken pity upon by the gods
Book Connection: Immortality refers to Janie's resilience, literally being the only one who lives throughout the entire book

The oak tree

Characteristics: strong and guiding
Greek Myth interpretation: Associated with Zeus, the leader of the gods
Book Connection: Oak trees characteristics are present in, and symbolize, how Jody is a leader for the town

The lemon tree

Characteristics: helpful and revitalizing
Greek Myth interpretation: In a myth involving Athena, the writer describes how lemons trees were “Happy to be a favorite and useful tree for everyone”
The lemon tree represents Teacake, who becomes apart of Janie’s “rebirth” after Jody dies, being a beacon of energy for everyone he is surrounded with

The pear tree

Characteristics: motherhood and fertility
Greek Myth interpretation: Homer states pears are “Gifts from the gods”
The pear tree is unattainable except in Janie’s fantasies, as there is no person who can be a perfect gift from the gods. Additionally, Janie never has kids, further emphasizing how Janie doesn’t achieve her young pear tree vision to “bloom.”
